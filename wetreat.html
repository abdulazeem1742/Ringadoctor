COLD & FLU

When you wake up sneezing, coughing, and have that achy, feverish, can't move a muscle feeling ,you may ask, is it a cold or the seasonal flu?

Cold and Flu both are caused by viruses. A cold is a milder respiratory illness and can make you feel bad for a few days. Common symptoms are runny nose - beginning with clear mucus that develops into thicker, green mucus as the cold progresses - blocked nose, sore throat, sneezing and cough. Cold is most contagious during the early stages, when the person has a runny nose and sore throat.

Whereas the flu can make you feel quite ill for a few days to weeks. Common symptoms are sudden fever of 38 - 40C (100 - 104F), muscle aches and pains, sweating, exhaustion and cough. Flu can also result in serious health problems such as pneumonia and hospitalizations. Whether a Cold or Flu, get medical help immediately and Ring a Doctor.

ALLERGIES

Allergies occur when your immune system reacts to a foreign substance - such as pollen, bee venom or pet dander - that doesn't cause a reaction in most people.

When you have allergies and you come into contact with the allergen, your immune system's reaction can inflame your skin, sinuses, airways or digestive system.

The severity of allergies varies from person to person and can range from minor irritation, rhinitis (runny nose and sneezing), eczema and other skin reactions, asthma to anaphylaxis - a potentially life-threatening emergency. Allergies are serious. Ring a Doctor to relieve your allergy symptoms.

ENT RELATED ISSUES

Certain conditions and diseases of the ear, nose, and throat can cause serious health problems. In some cases, especially if they are not detected and treated early, diseases such as cancer and infection involving the ear, nose, and throat can cause death. Most conditions of the ear, nose, and throat, however, can be effectively treated or managed.Common problems are blocked nose or nasal discharge, sore throat, ear pain or discharge, vertigo, pressure or swelling in ears or foreign body in nose or ear. Ring a Doctor if you are experiencing problems with your ear(s), nose and throat.

SKIN DISEASES

Did you know that your skin is the largest organ of your body and it separates the inside of your body from the outside world? It protects from bacteria and viruses, and regulates body temperature. Conditions that irritate, clog, or inflame your skin can cause symptoms such as redness, swelling, burning, and itching. Allergies, irritants, your genetic makeup, and certain diseases and immune system problems can cause dermatitis, hives, and other skin conditions. Skin can also develop several kinds of cancers. Ring a Doctor to figure out what is happening with your skin and how to treat it.

While we all feel sad, moody or low from time to time, some people experience these feelings intensely, for long periods of time (weeks, months or even years) and sometimes without any apparent reason. Depression is more than just a low mood. It's a serious condition that affects your physical and mental health.

nxiety is more than just feeling stressed or worried. GASTRIC DISEASES

Most people suffer from gastrointestinal problems but they don't like to talk about it. There is no need to suffer in silence. Some of the most common digestive conditions and their symptoms are mentioned here, if you suspect you have any of these issues than don't delay in calling your doctor.

Chest Pain or Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD)

When stomach acid backs up into your esophagus - a condition called acid reflux - you may feel a burning pain in the middle of your chest. It often occurs after meals or at night. If you experience persistent heartburn, bad breath, tooth erosion, nausea, pain in your chest or upper part of your abdomen, or have trouble swallowing or breathing, call your doctor.

Celiac Disease

Celiac disease is a serious sensitivity to gluten, a protein found in wheat, rye, and barley. It damages your villi, the finger-like protrusions in small intestines that help to absorb nutrients from the foods we eat. Symptoms of celiac disease in kids include abdominal pain and bloating, diarrhea, constipation, vomiting, and weight loss. Symptoms in adults also can include anemia, fatigue, bone loss, depression, and seizures.
